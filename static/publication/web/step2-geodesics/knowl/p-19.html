Section 5 wraps up the proof of Theorem 1.1 using the conclusions of the previous sections. This section is where the strict convexity of the norm is critical. The importance of the assumption is that any linear map has a unique maximum on the ball. By observing that any element of \(\ker B\) defines an invariant along the corresponding optimal control, the uniqueness is exploited to prove that infinite geodesics must be invariant under blowdowns. Corollary 1.2 follows from the sub-Finsler case by the observation that the length metric associated with a homogeneous norm is always a sub-Finsler metric.
