Finally we give two applications of the parametrization of positive gradings. In Proposition 4.10 we show that all non-equivalent positive gradings define non-isomorphic Heintze groups. Thus we give methods to enumerate diagonal Heintze groups. Consequently, we make progress on the problem of finding all Heintze groups with prescribed nilradical, which is a question already hinted by Heintze, see the discussion after Theorem 3 in [19].
