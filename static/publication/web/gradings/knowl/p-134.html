If the base field is not algebraically closed, we need to explicitly require that the constructed elements of \(C(\mathfrak{t})\setminus\mathfrak{t}\) are diagonalizable. The subset of diagonalizable elements of \(C(\mathfrak{t})\setminus\mathfrak{t}\) is a semialgebraic set, and constructions to extract points from such sets exist, see for instance SectionÂ 13 of [1] on the existential theory of the reals. The problem is that these methods are practical only in low dimensions, and the construction would be needed in dimension \(\dim\mathfrak{gl}(\mathfrak{g})=\dim(\mathfrak{g})^2\text{.}\) For Lie algebras defined over finite fields, more efficient randomized algorithms to find split tori exist, see [5] and [27].
