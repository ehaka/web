We do not in general require that the weights of an \(A\)-grading generate the grading group \(A\) in order to include e.g. gradings over \(A=\mathbb{R}\) in the discussion. Moreover, weights of a grading may have additional relations coming from the ambient group structure, even when the corresponding layers are unrelated, see for instance ExampleÂ 3.13. To build a satisfactory theory using homomorphisms between grading groups, we consider the notion of an (abelian) universal realization, see Section 3.3 of [22].
