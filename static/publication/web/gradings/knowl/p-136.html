Consider the Lie algebra \(L_{6,22}(1)\) in [2] with basis \(\{X_1,\dots,X_6 \}\text{,}\) where the only non-zero bracket relations are

\begin{equation*} [X_1,X_2]=X_5,\;\; [X_1,X_3]=X_6,\;\; [X_2,X_4]=X_6,\;\; [X_3,X_4]=X_5\text{.} \end{equation*}

In a basis \(\{Y_1,\dots,Y_6 \}\) adapted to the maximal grading, the bracket relations are

\begin{equation*} [Y_1,Y_2]=Y_3,\;\;[Y_4,Y_5]=Y_6\text{.} \end{equation*}

From these bracket relations one sees more easily that the Lie algebra \(L_{6,22}(1)\) is isomorphic to \(L_{3,2} \times L_{3,2}\text{,}\) where \(L_{3,2}\) is the first Heisenberg Lie algebra.
