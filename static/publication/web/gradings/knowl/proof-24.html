Proof of correctness.

Let \(P\) be as in (A.1). Lemma A.3 implies that step 2 constructs all the extremal points of \(P\text{.}\) By Lemma A.2, if no extremal points are found, the set \(P\) is empty and no positive realization exists. We still need to argue that if \(P\) is non-empty, then a positive realization exists.

A priori, even if some \(\mathbf{x} = B^{-1}(\mathbf{b}) \ge 0\text{,}\) the corresponding weights \(x_i+1,\ldots,x_k+1\) do not necessarily define a realization of the original grading, since in general these weights are non-distinct and hence define a [cross-reference to target(s) "def-same-layers" missing] grading. By Lemma 2.10 this coarser grading is a push-forward grading of the universal realization of the original grading, via a homomorphism from some \(\mathbb{Z}^m\) to \(\mathbb{R}\) mapping the weights to \(x_1+1,\ldots,x_k+1\text{.}\) The homomorphism is realized as a projection to some line of \(\mathbb{R}^m\text{,}\) as in the proof of [cross-reference to target(s) "prop-Z-gradings-to-positive-forward" missing]. By perturbing this line, it is always possible to find another homomorphism that is injective on the weights and maps all the weights to strictly positive reals. Hence there is also a positive realization of the original grading.
