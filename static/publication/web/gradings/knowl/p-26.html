The independence of the base field is particularly convenient because it allows us to make use of computer algebra systems, where it is necessary to work with a computable field, i.e., it is necessary to be able to distinguish elements and compute all the field operations with finite algorithms. For instance, although the field of reals \(\mathbb{R}\) is not computable, we may make use of our computer algebra implementation as soon as the Lie algebra is defined over the rationals or the real algebraic numbers, i.e., the real numbers that are algebraic over the rationals.
