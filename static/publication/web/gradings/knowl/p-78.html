It follows from Proposition 2.19 and the discussion in Section 3.6 of [23] that maximal gradings are universal realizations of fine gradings. In Definition 3.18 of [6], maximal gradings are defined as the gradings induced by maximal split tori in the automorphism group \(\Aut(\mathfrak{g})\text{.}\) Proposition 3.20 of [6] states that maximal gradings in the sense of [6] have a universal property equivalent to Proposition 2.19, so by Lemma 2.6 any such grading is maximal also in the sense of Definition 2.17. The maximal gradings considered in [16] are the gradings induced by maximal split tori.
