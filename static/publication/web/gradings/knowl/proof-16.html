Proof of Proposition 4.10.

Rescaling the derivations by a scalar, we may assume that the smallest of the eigenvalues for both derivations is 1. Since the Heintze groups are assumed to be isomorphic, it is straightforward to see that there is a vector \(X \in \mathfrak{g}\) such that the derivation \(\alpha\) is conjugate by a Lie algebra automorphism of \(\mathfrak{g}\) to the derivation \(\beta + \ad_X\text{.}\) By Lemma 4.13, it follows that \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) are conjugate. Applying Lemma 2.15i to the split tori spanned by \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) gives the desired result.
