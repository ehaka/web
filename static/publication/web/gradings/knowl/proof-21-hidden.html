Rescaling the derivations by a scalar, we may assume the smallest of the eigenvalues for both the derivations to be 1. Since the Heintze groups are assumed to be isomorphic, it is straightforward to see that there is a vector \(X \in \mathfrak{g}\) so that the derivation \(\alpha\) is conjugate by a Lie algebra automorphism of \(\mathfrak{g}\) to the derivation \(\beta + \ad_X\text{.}\) We use Lemma 4.13 to find that actually \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) are conjugate. Applying Lemma 2.14i to the split tori spanned by \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) gives the desired result.
