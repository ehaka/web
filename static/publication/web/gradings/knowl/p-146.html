The main part of the classification is the construction of a maximal grading (Algorithm 3.11) and the enumeration of torsion-free gradings (Proposition 3.1). Since all the Lie algebras we consider are defined over the algebraic numbers, we are free to make use of our computer algebra implementation, as discussed in Subsection 2.1. As a starting point we used the classifications of nilpotent Lie algebras given in [12] for dimensions less than 6, [2] for dimension 6, and [18] for dimension 7. The classification up to dimension 6 has a pre-existing computer implementation in the GAP package [3]. Since these Lie algebras are not always given in a basis adapted to any maximal grading, we first compute the maximal grading using the methods described in Subsection 3.4 and switch to a basis adapted to the resulting grading.
