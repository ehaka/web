The most central of the implemented algorithms is the construction of the maximal grading of a Lie algebra \(\mathfrak{g}\text{,}\) where we assume that the base field \(F\) of \(\mathfrak{g}\) is algebraically closed (see Algorithm 3.11). Given a maximal grading, one is able to construct a positive grading or determine that one does not exist (see TODO: turn the paragraph into a remark). We also give an explicit construction for a stratification or a decision that one does not exist (Algorithm 3.5), even when the base field \(F\) is not assumed to be algebraically closed. The algorithms for positive gradings and stratifications rely on the characterizations of existence of a positive grading in Proposition 3.22 of [6] and stratifiability in Lemma 3.10 of [6].
