Definition 2.7.

Let \((X,d)\) be a metric space. Let \(\Omega\) be the space of rectifiable curves of \(X\) and let \(\ell_d\colon\Omega\to \RR\) be the length functional. For points \(x,y\in X\text{,}\) denote by \(\Omega(x,y)\subset\Omega\) the space of all rectifiable curves connecting the points \(x\) and \(y\text{.}\) The length metric associated to the metric \(d\) is the map \(d_\ell\colon X\times X\to\RR\cup\{\infty\}\) defined by

\begin{equation*} d_\ell(x,y) := \inf \{\ell_d(\gamma): \gamma\in \Omega(x,y) \}\text{.} \end{equation*}

If \(d=d_\ell\text{,}\) then the metric \(d\) is called a length metric.
