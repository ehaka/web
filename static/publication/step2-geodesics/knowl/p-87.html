For almost every \(t\text{,}\) the linear map \(a(t)\) is a subdifferential of the squared norm \(\frac{1}{2}\norm{\cdot}^2\) at the point \(u(t)\text{.}\) Since \(\norm{u(t)}\equiv 1\) is constant, continuity of the curve \(a\) and Lemma 2.19 imply the bound \(\abs{a(t)X} \leq \norm{X}\) for every \(t\in[0,T]\text{.}\) The identities (4.1) and (4.2) then imply the desired conclusion that

\begin{equation*} \lim\limits_{T\to\infty}\abs{B\left(\intavg_{0}^Tu(t)\,dt,X\right)} \leq \lim\limits_{T\to\infty}\frac{2}{T}\norm{X} = 0\text{.} \end{equation*}
