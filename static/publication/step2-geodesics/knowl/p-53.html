Theorem 24.7 of [21] shows (among other things) that since the set of points \(\mathcal{S}=\{Y_i: i\in\NN\}\cup \{Y\}\) is compact, the family of subdifferentials

\begin{equation*} \partial E(\mathcal{S}):= \bigcup_{X\in \mathcal{S}}\{a\in \partial E(X) \} \end{equation*}

is also compact. Hence there exists a converging subsequence \(a_k\to a\in\partial E(\mathcal{S})\text{.}\) The claim is concluded by Theorem 24.4 of [21], which shows that the convergences \(Y_k\to Y\) and \(a_k\to a\) with \(a_k\in\partial E(Y_k)\) imply that \(a\in\partial E(Y)\text{.}\)
