(ii).

The claim follows from Remark 3.13 of [13]. The point is that by weak compactness of closed balls in \(\Lloc([0,\infty);V_1)\) there exists a weakly convergent subsequence \(u_\dilationfactor \rightharpoonup v\) to some \(v\in\Lloc([0,\infty);V_1)\text{.}\) The definitions of control and weak compactness imply that \(v\) is a control for \(\tilde{\gamma}\text{,}\) so in particular \(\tilde{u}(t)=v(t)\) for almost every \(t\text{.}\) Finally, the geodesic assumption implies that \(\norm{u(t)}\equiv 1 \equiv \norm{\tilde{u}(t)}\text{,}\) so the weak convergence is upgraded to strong convergence \(u_{\dilationfactor}\to \tilde{u}\) in \(\Lloc([0,\infty);V_1)\text{.}\)
