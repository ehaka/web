The expression for the symplectic form is more intricate. By definition the canonical symplectic form \(\omega\) on the cotangent bundle \(T^*G\)Â is the differential of the tautological one-form \(\sum_{i=1}^n a_i\theta_i\text{.}\) That is, in left-trivialized coordinates, the symplectic form has the expression (see e.g. Section 4.2 of [1] for more details)

\begin{equation} \omega = \sum_{i=1}^nda_i\wedge \theta_i + \sum_{i=1}^na_id\theta_i. \tag{3.10} \end{equation}

The differentials \(d\theta_i\) can be evaluated along vector fields \(X,Y\in\Gamma(TG)\) by the classical formula

\begin{equation*} d\theta_i(X,Y) = X\theta_i(Y)-Y\theta_i(X)-\theta_i([X,Y])\text{.} \end{equation*}

For left-invariant vector fields this reduces to \(d\theta_i(X,Y)=-\theta_i([X,Y])\text{.}\)
