Remark 4.11.

The enumeration of Heintze groups has a few caveats:

  1. Already over \(\mathfrak{g}=\mathbb{R}^2\) there are uncountably many isomorphism classes of Heintze groups due to the multitude of possible projections \(\mathbb{Z}^2 \to \mathbb{R}\text{.}\) The same is true more generally whenever the maximal torus has dimension at least 2.
  2. Our methods are in general able to find maximal gradings only for Lie algebras over algebraically closed fields. On the contrary, the base field of Heintze groups is \(\mathbb{R}\text{.}\)
